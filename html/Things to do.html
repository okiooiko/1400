Camping & picnic

picnic area

Camping is available at The Settlement Camping Area on Springbrook Plateau. There are dedicated camping and picnic areas in the park. Camping permits must be reserved in advance by mail or phone.

Book your campsite by phone or email.

Read about the essentials to bring with you when camping in Springbrook National Park before your visit.

Camping is not permitted anywhere in Springbrook National Park except at The Settlement camping area. For specific instructions, please refer to: looking after informantion

Hiking

Hiking

Springbrook National Park offers a variety of walking opportunities, ranging in length from 300 meters to 54 kilometers. If you are interested in doing this 54km walk, please read the walk details ahead of time so you can better plan your itinerary.

Many people do night hikes in the Natural Bridges section of Springbrook National Park to spot fireflies. If you are hiking at night, please pay attention to safety and obey the rules of the park. Bring communication gear in case you are in danger or get lost. If this happens, please contact us: 0712345678

View Wildlife

Blue bugs

The park is dominated by subtropical rainforests, ancient Antarctic beech, hoop pine, eucalyptus forests and mountains that provide shelter for a wide variety of wildlife. More than 100 species of birds live in the park. Spring creeks provide an important sanctuary for different songbirds.

The abundant water resources in the park have created a variety of aquatic animals. Frogs are the most vocal, and blue spiny crayfish are the most colorful.

The blue worms at night are very shocking and beautiful. These blue worms inhabit only parts of Australia and New Zealand.
