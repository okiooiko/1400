Springbrook Plateau

Springbrook Plateau

Several vantage points on the Springbrook Plateau offer expansive views of the surrounding mountains and coastline. The artificially constructed lookouts ensure safety while viewing, and can be reached within a short walk. But even on sunny days, these lookouts are often obscured by clouds. For the best views, you'd better visit on a clear, fog-free day.

There is also a place where many of Springbrook's stories are recorded called Gauriemabah. This is Springbrook's first school building established in 1911. If interested you can search online for more information on Springbrook's history. Gauriemabah is open every weekday from 8:00 am to 3:30 pm. The center has no staff, if you have any questions, please contact us: 0712345678

Natural Bridge

Natrual bridge

Natural bridges, waterfalls and vaulted caves are surrounded by subtropical rainforest in the beautiful Numinba Valley. Beneath the western walls of the Springbrook Plateau, lush subtropical rainforest does not overshadow the beauty of the area's caves and rock bridges.

A display stand at the entrance to the sightseeing trail displays maps and information about the park and its wildlife. Parts of Cave Creek within the National Park Natural Bridge are restricted access areas and swimming is also prohibited. If found, you will be punished. This restriction is intended to protect the sensitive ecosystem of the fireflies and creek, and to maximize visitor safety.

To read more about Natural Bridge's restricted access areas, please click: Natural Bridge Restricted Access Area sign

Note: Cell phone signal in this area is very poor. Be careful

Mount Cougal

Mount Cougal

Mount Cougal's twin peaks are named after a wilderness area dominated by subtropical rainforest and rocky riverbeds. Waterfalls are a scenic park feature. The creek contains submerged logs and rocks. Water depth is inconsistent and unpredictable. The rocks are covered with moss and algae, and even though they look dry, they are actually smooth. There are many people who have accidents here, and their bodies have irreversible damage.

A display stand at the entrance to the track displays maps and information about the park and its wildlife. Be sure to pay attention to the park's hazard signs. For details, please read: Natural Bridge Restricted Access Area sign

Note: Cell phone signal in this area is very poor. Please always pay attention to safety
