Springbrook National Park is one of the national parks in eastern Australia, located south of Brisbane, Queensland, and is part of the Australian World Heritage Site "East Coast Rainforest Reserve". The most famous of Springbrook National Park is the Natural Bridge, where a natural cave is formed due to the erosion of water, which resembles a bridge, so it is called a natural bridge.
